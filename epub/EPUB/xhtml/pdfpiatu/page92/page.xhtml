Descriptive and expository paragraphs

 

Descriptive paragraphs describe in detail a person, a thing, a place or any other object of description. To

 

write a descriptive paragraph you must rely on your powers of observation and on imagery. The powers

 

of observation mean your ability to observe and watch your object of study. Pay attention to the size,

 

color, shape, material, effects of light on it, texture, etc. Imagery is figurative language based on the five

 

senses: sight, smell, taste, touch and hearing. In this way your reader can be able to imagine completely

 

what you painted with words. This is commonly found in literary texts.

 

High above the city, on a tall column, stood the statue of the Happy Prince. He was gilded

 

all over with thin leaves of fine gold, for eyes he had two bright sapphires, and a large red

 

ruby glowed on his sword-hilt. He was very much admired indeed.

 

Expository paragraphs present important information about a specific subject. In this case you need

 

to find out truthful information and the style has to be clear and direct, without metaphors or any

 

written information that might be confusing. This type of text is usually found in textbooks, newspapers,

 

encyclopedias, etc.
