Narrative paragraphs

 

A narrative text tells a story. It must be clearly divided into beginning, middle and ending. The story is

 

about a main character and he / she usually has an enemy who makes life difficult for him / her. There is

 

also another character who is a friend and always helps the main character. The main character suffers a

 

change and at the end he / she is not the same that he / she was at the beginning. Once you have the

 

structure, use imagery to make the setting–where everything takes place–more interesting. There can be

 

more simple narrations that just tell an anecdote.

 

Once upon a time there was a young sailor who had widowed a year ago. One night, he was

 

fishing near a small island and he heard some singing. He crept carefully to watch what was

 

happening and he saw a group of beautiful women dancing around a fire. Suddenly he felt wet

 

clothes under his hand, but they weren’t clothes. When he looked closer, he realized they were

 

seal skins. The women were selkies! Selkies are magical seals that when they come to land

 

by night turn into women. They are loyal and affectionate. The sailor took one skin and waited.

 

Almost at dawn the women dressed in the seal skins and jumped into the water. All, but one.

 

She was looking around with sad eyes. The sailor showed her, her skin and negotiated with

 

her. “Marry me and I’ll give you your skin in ten years.” She accepted. She was sad at the

 

beginning. In time she was pregnant. She gave birth to two beautiful children in ten years

 

of dutiful marriage. She was a kind and loyal wife, but she didn’t love her husband because

 

of the way he had trapped her. After ten years, she asked for her skin. “You promised,” she

 

said. The sailor kept his promise and gave her, her skin back. That night she dressed her

 

skin and jumped into the water, but she took her children with her. As soon as they touched

 

the water, they became beautiful seals. The sailor wept and felt lonelier than ever.
