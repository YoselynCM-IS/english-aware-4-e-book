“T

 

he Murders in the Rue Morgue” is a murder-mystery

 

short story by Edgar Allen Poe. It has been described

 

by critics as the first modern detective story, and was

 

first published in Graham’s Magazine in 1841. The story follows

 

two men, an unnamed narrator and an amateur detective with

 

powerful deductive reasoning skills named Auguste Dupin,

 

referred to throughout the story simply as Dupin. Dupin and

 

the narrator become obsessed with solving the brutal murder of

 

two women, a mother and a daughter, after reading accounts of

 

it in the paper. Ultimately, Dupin reaches out to G-, the head of

 

police, to help him solve the case after the police arrest someone

 

whom Dupin believes to be innocent.
